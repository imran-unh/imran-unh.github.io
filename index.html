About

My work combines satellite remote sensing, geospatial analysis, and hydrologic/hazard modeling to characterize glacial lake evolution and flood risks under climate change.

Current Research Focus

1) SAR-based glacial lake mapping & monitoring

Developing robust workflows for mapping glacial lakes using Sentinel-1 SAR, improving monitoring in cloud-prone, high-relief mountain environments.

2) Seasonal supraglacial lake dynamics

Multi-year analysis of supraglacial lake seasonal behavior in the Karakoram, linking melt-season conditions, persistence, and drainage patterns to broader climate signals.

3) Glacier–lake–climate interactions

Examining how lake evolution relates to glacier dynamics, debris cover, and climatic forcing— with implications for downstream impacts and hazard assessment.

4) Outburst flood (GLOF) hazard modeling

Modeling potential outburst flood scenarios (e.g., channel hydraulics and flow routing) to support risk communication and disaster preparedness in vulnerable regions.
